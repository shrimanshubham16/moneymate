# Getting Started with FinFlow

Welcome to FinFlow! This guide will help you get started in just a few minutes.

## What is FinFlow?

FinFlow is a free, privacy-first personal finance app that shows you your financial health at a glance. Add your income, expenses, and investments, and get a real-time health score that tells you exactly what you'll have left at month-end.

## 🚀 Quick Start (5 Minutes)

### Step 1: Create Your Account

1. Visit [FinFlow](https://freefinflow.vercel.app/)
2. Click **"Sign Up"**
3. Choose a **username** (⚠️ **permanent** - choose wisely!)
4. Set a **strong password** (min 8 characters, uppercase, number, special char)
5. Click **"Create Account"**

⚠️ **Important**: 
- Your username cannot be changed later
- **Save your recovery key** - you'll need it to reset your password
- No email recovery option - keep your recovery key safe!

### Step 2: Add Your Income

1. Go to **Settings** → **Plan Finances** → **Income**
2. Click **"Add New Income"**
3. Enter:
   - **Name**: e.g., "Monthly Salary"
   - **Amount**: Your monthly income
   - **Frequency**: Monthly, Quarterly, or Yearly
4. Click **"Add Income"**

💡 **Tip**: Add all income sources (salary, freelance, rental income, RSUs, etc.)

### Step 3: Add Fixed Expenses

Fixed expenses are predictable monthly costs (rent, subscriptions, EMIs).

1. Go to **Settings** → **Plan Finances** → **Fixed Expenses**
2. Click **"Add Fixed Expense"**
3. Enter:
   - **Name**: e.g., "Rent"
   - **Amount**: Monthly amount
   - **Category**: Select from dropdown
   - **Mark as SIP**: If it's an investment (mutual fund, PPF, etc.)
4. Click **"Add Expense"**

### Step 4: Add Variable Expenses

Variable expenses are flexible spending (groceries, entertainment, dining).

1. Go to **Settings** → **Plan Finances** → **Variable Expenses**
2. Click **"Add Variable Expense"**
3. Enter:
   - **Name**: e.g., "Groceries"
   - **Budget**: Monthly budget limit
   - **Category**: Select from dropdown
4. Click **"Add Expense"**

### Step 5: Check Your Health Score

1. Go to **Dashboard**
2. See your **Financial Health Score** at the top
3. This shows what you'll have left at month-end

🎉 **That's it!** You're all set up.

## 📊 Understanding Your Health Score

Your **Financial Health Score** is calculated as:

```
Health = Total Income - (Fixed Expenses + Variable Expenses + Investments + Dues)
```

- **Positive Score**: You'll have money left over
- **Negative Score**: You're spending more than you earn
- **Zero**: Breaking even
