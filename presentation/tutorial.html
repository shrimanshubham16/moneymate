4 Fixed Expenses

Adding Fixed Expenses

Screenshot: Fixed Expenses Page - Add Form
Show the fixed expenses page with the add expense form

Fixed Expenses - Add recurring bills

  1. Navigate to Dashboard ‚Üí Fixed Expenses (or Settings ‚Üí Plan Finances ‚Üí Fixed Expenses)
  2. Click "Add Fixed Expense"
  3. Fill in the details:
    • Name: e.g., "Netflix", "Rent", "Internet"
    • Amount: The expense amount
    • Category: Select from dropdown (Utilities, Entertainment, Loan, etc.)
    • Frequency: Monthly, Quarterly, Yearly
    • SIP Toggle: Enable for quarterly/yearly expenses to auto-save monthly
  4. Click "Save"

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) for Periodic Expenses

For expenses that occur quarterly or yearly (like insurance, property tax), enable SIP:

  1. When adding/editing a fixed expense, toggle "Enable SIP"
  2. FinFlow will automatically calculate how much to save monthly
  3. Example: ‚Çπ12,000 yearly insurance ‚Üí Save ‚Çπ1,000/month
  4. View all SIP expenses in Dashboard ‚Üí SIP for Periodic
Screenshot: Fixed Expense with SIP Enabled

SIP Feature - Auto-save for periodic expenses

Managing Fixed Expenses

  1. View all fixed expenses as cards on the page
  2. Click Edit icon (top-right of card) to update
  3. Click Delete icon to remove
  4. Expenses with category "Loan" automatically appear in the Loans widget
Fixed expenses are automatically included in your health score calculation. Make sure to add all recurring bills!

5 Variable Expenses

Planning Variable Expenses

Screenshot: Variable Expenses Page - Plan View
Show the variable expenses page with plans and actuals

Variable Expenses - Plan and track your spending

  1. Go to Dashboard ‚Üí Variable Expenses
  2. Click "Add Plan" to create a spending plan
  3. Fill in:
    • Category: Food, Shopping, Travel, etc.
    • Planned Amount: Your monthly budget
    • Goal: Optional goal amount
  4. Click "Save"

Recording Actual Expenses

  1. Click "Add Actual" button
  2. Fill in the expense details:
    • Category: Select from your plans
    • Subcategory: Optional (e.g., "Groceries" under "Food")
    • Amount: Amount spent
    • Payment Mode: UPI/Cash, Extra Cash, or Credit Card
  3. Click "Save"
Payment Modes:
  • UPI/Cash: Reduces available funds immediately
  • Extra Cash: Doesn't reduce available funds (for tracking only)
  • Credit Card: Will be billed on credit card billing date

8 Understanding Health Score

What is Health Score?

The Health Score shows how much money you'll have remaining after all expenses, investments, and bills.

Screenshot: Health Details Page
Show the detailed health breakdown page with all components

Health Details - Complete financial breakdown

How Health Score is Calculated

  1. Available Funds: Total income minus fixed expenses, investments, and variable expenses
  2. Remaining: Available funds minus unpaid credit card bills
  3. Health Score: The remaining amount (displayed as integer)

Viewing Detailed Breakdown

  1. Click on the Health Indicator on the Dashboard
  2. Or navigate to Dashboard ‚Üí Health (if available)
  3. View detailed breakdown:
    • Income sources
    • Fixed expenses (with prorated amounts)
    • Variable expenses (planned vs actual)
    • Investments
    • Credit card bills
    • Final remaining amount
Health score updates in real-time as you add/modify expenses, income, or investments. The score is displayed as an integer (no decimals).

11 Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Health Score Mismatch: If you see different health scores on dashboard and health page, refresh the page. Both use the same calculation method.
Can't Login: Make sure you're using the correct username and password. Username is case-sensitive.
Expenses Not Showing: Check if you've selected the correct billing cycle in preferences. Expenses are calculated based on your billing cycle.
Credit Card Not Updating: Make sure to click "Update Bill" after modifying the bill amount. The form saves the card details, but you need to explicitly update the bill.
